Welcome to the Diabetes Prediction Tool

This tool uses a predictive model to assess the likelihood of diabetes based on several input factors. Below are the factors used by the model to make predictions:

  • Pregnancies: The number of times the individual has been pregnant.
  • Glucose: The plasma glucose concentration a 2 hours in an oral glucose tolerance test.
  • Blood Pressure: Diastolic blood pressure (mm Hg).
  • Skin Thickness: Triceps skin fold thickness (mm).
  • Insulin: 2-Hour serum insulin (mu U/ml).
  • BMI: Body mass index (weight in kg/(height in m)^2).
  • Diabetes Pedigree Function: A function that scores the likelihood of diabetes based on family history.
  • Age: Age in years.

The model has been trained on a dataset of individuals with known diabetes outcomes and uses this information to make predictions about new data. It's designed to help individuals assess their risk and encourage those with high-risk predictions to seek professional medical advice.

This tool is intended for informational purposes only and is not a substitute for professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment.
